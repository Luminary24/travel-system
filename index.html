Section 01

World Travel Planning

Planning a round-the-world trip is one of the most complex and rewarding logistical puzzles you'll ever solve. Get these foundational decisions right and everything else flows naturally.

Key Planning Steps

1

Define Your Travel Style & Budget

Before booking anything, decide whether you're backpacking on a shoestring, travelling comfortably mid-range, or indulging in luxury experiences. Your daily budget is the compass for every decision that follows. Be brutally honest about what you need to be happy.

2

Choose Destinations & Build a Direction

Round-the-world trips work best when you travel in one direction — either eastward (Europe → Asia → Americas) or westward. Make a dream list of 15–20 places, then cut it to 8–12 that are geographically logical. Less is almost always more.

3

Map Seasons & Weather Windows

Southeast Asia's rainy season (June–October), Europe's summer crowds (July–August), and South America's winter (June–September) all dramatically affect your experience and costs. Time your arrival in each region to hit shoulder season — the sweet spot of good weather and lower prices.

4

Decide: RTW Ticket vs. Book-As-You-Go

Round-the-World airline alliance tickets (Oneworld, Star Alliance) offer 3–6 stops from approx. $3,500–$6,000 USD but lock you into dates. Booking independently is often cheaper and far more flexible — ideal for trips longer than 3 months. Many seasoned travellers prefer booking each leg 4–8 weeks ahead.

5

Sort Logistics Before You Go

Secure all necessary visas (some require weeks), organise a travel credit card with no foreign transaction fees (e.g. Wise, Revolut, Charles Schwab), get comprehensive travel insurance, and set up online banking access with notifications. Notify your home bank of your travel dates.

6

Pack Lightly — Your Back Will Thank You

The golden rule: if your bag weighs over 10–12 kg (carry-on), you've packed too much. You can buy almost anything on the road. A lighter pack means cheaper flights (no check-in fees), faster movement, and far less stress. One carry-on and a day pack is the optimal setup for most long-term travellers.

🌦 Seasonal Sweet Spots

  • SE Asia: Nov–Feb (dry, cool)
  • Europe: Apr–Jun & Sep–Oct (shoulder)
  • Japan: Mar–Apr (sakura) or Oct–Nov
  • East Africa: Jan–Mar or Jun–Oct (dry)
  • South America: Apr–Jun (shoulder in most)
  • India: Oct–Mar (cooler & drier north)

💳 Visa Considerations

  • Visa on arrival: Thailand, Nepal, Cambodia, most of SE Asia
  • e-Visa: India, Turkey, Kenya (apply 1–4 weeks ahead)
  • Pre-application required: China (≥4 weeks), Russia, Brazil
  • Free entry: EU/Schengen for many, most of Latin America
  • Always check current requirements — policies change frequently

📋 Essential Pre-Departure Checklist

  • Passport valid 6+ months beyond return date
  • Travel insurance covering medical evacuation
  • Unlocked phone + local SIM strategy
  • Scanned copies of all documents stored online
  • Emergency fund of $500–1,000 separate from main budget
  • Vaccinations checked (Yellow Fever for Africa; Hep A/B, Typhoid for Asia)

Section 02

Budgeting Framework

How much does world travel actually cost? The honest answer is: it varies wildly depending on your choices. Here's how to estimate realistically and understand the full picture.

Traveller Budget Tiers (Average Daily Spend)

🎒
Budget Backpacker
$25 – $60 /day

Hostel dorms, street food, slow travel, local transport

  • Dormitory hostels or budget guesthouses
  • Street food and local eateries
  • Buses, trains, and shared transport
  • Free attractions and hikes
  • Cook some meals yourself
  • Best in SE Asia, Eastern Europe, Central America
🧳
Mid-Range Traveller
$80 – $175 /day

Private rooms, sit-down restaurants, mix of transport

  • Private hotel rooms or Airbnb
  • Restaurants (not fine dining)
  • Occasional flights for long legs
  • Guided tours and paid activities
  • Budget airlines for regional hops
  • Suitable for most regions comfortably
✨
Luxury Traveller
$250 – $600+ /day

Boutique hotels, fine dining, private transfers

  • Boutique hotels and resorts (4–5 star)
  • Fine dining and cocktail bars
  • Business / premium class flights
  • Private tours and experiences
  • Spa, taxis, concierge services
  • Anywhere in the world is accessible

💡 The Smart Sequencing Strategy

Many experienced round-the-world travellers start in expensive regions (Western Europe, Japan) while their energy and excitement are highest, then transition to more affordable regions (SE Asia, South America) where a modest budget stretches much further. This approach also means you spend less time "budget-watching" when you're most likely to be jet-lagged and disoriented at the start.

Section 05

Money-Saving Strategies

Experienced travellers save 30–50% compared to first-timers — not through discomfort, but through smart habits and knowing where costs hide.

✈ Cheap Flights

Flight Booking Mastery

  • Use Google Flights' calendar view to find cheapest travel dates — flexible dates save 20–40%
  • Book 6–8 weeks ahead for international flights — not too early, not last minute
  • Use Skyscanner's "Everywhere" search when you have flexibility on destination
  • Consider flying into smaller nearby airports (Stansted vs Heathrow, Oakland vs SFO)
  • Budget airlines (AirAsia, Ryanair, EasyJet) for regional hops — check baggage fees carefully
  • Incognito browsing mode can avoid dynamic price increases from repeated searches
  • Set price alerts on Google Flights and Kayak for target routes
🏨 Accommodation Hacks

Sleep Cheaper, Sleep Better

  • Hostels often have excellent private rooms at half hotel prices — don't assume hostel = dorm
  • Book 1–2 weeks ahead in budget regions; 4–8 weeks ahead in Europe/Japan in peak season
  • Couchsurfing.com is free and offers genuinely memorable local connections
  • Workaway, HelpX, WWOOF: 4–5 hours work/day in exchange for free bed and food
  • Monthly Airbnb rates are 30–50% cheaper than nightly — commit to longer stays
  • House-sitting (TrustedHousesitters) is free accommodation in exchange for pet care
  • Stay in residential neighbourhoods, not tourist zones — 30–50% cheaper and more authentic
🚌 Local Transport

Get Around for Less

  • Night buses and night trains save both transport cost and a night's accommodation
  • Grab and inDrive apps give transparent ride pricing in SE Asia — always cheaper than flagged taxis
  • Multi-day or weekly transit passes are almost always better value than single tickets
  • Renting a scooter ($5–10/day in SE Asia) is transformative for exploration — check insurance
  • BlaBlaCar is excellent in Europe for intercity travel at 50–70% cheaper than trains
  • Walking with Maps.me offline maps is free and often fastest through city centres
🍽 Food & Drink

Eat Well, Spend Little

  • Eat where locals eat — if there's an English menu outside, expect tourist prices
  • Markets, supermarkets, and street stalls are almost always 50–80% cheaper than restaurants
  • Lunch is almost always cheaper than dinner for the same dish — dine out at lunch
  • Carry a reusable water bottle with a filter (LifeStraw, Sawyer) — saves $2–4/day on bottled water
  • Happy hours (17:00–19:00) in SE Asia offer buy-1-get-1 beers
  • Supermarket shopping for breakfast saves dramatically vs café breakfasts
💳 Money & Banking

Avoid Fees & Hidden Costs

  • Wise or Revolut cards: zero (or near-zero) FX conversion fees — essential for long-term travel
  • Charles Schwab (US) and Starling (UK) refund all ATM fees globally — carry as backup
  • Always choose "local currency" when asked by ATMs — the DCC option is a 3–7% rip-off
  • Withdraw larger amounts less frequently — most ATMs charge a flat fee per withdrawal
  • Carry $100–200 USD cash as emergency backup in regions with unreliable ATM networks
  • Track spending daily with Trail Wallet or TravelSpend apps — small leaks add up fast
🛡 Insurance & Health

Protect Yourself Wisely

  • SafetyWing is the go-to for long-term travellers at $45–60/month — rolling monthly coverage
  • World Nomads is better for high-adventure activities (diving, skiing, climbing)
  • Document all valuable items before departure — serial numbers, photos, receipts
  • EHIC/GHIC card (for Europeans in Europe) gives free public healthcare — get one free
  • See a travel medicine clinic 6–8 weeks before departure for vaccine advice
  • Pharmacies in SE Asia and South Asia have medications OTC that require prescriptions elsewhere
🚨 Hidden Costs to Watch Out For

Tourist taxes (paid at hotels), resort fees, luggage fees on budget airlines, tourist vs resident pricing at attractions, expensive airport transfers, roaming data charges, mandatory guided tours at certain UNESCO sites, and tipping customs that don't exist back home — these "invisible" costs can add 10–20% to your actual daily spend. Always read the fine print and check TripAdvisor forums for up-to-date "gotcha" warnings at each destination.

Timing Is Everything

Best Time to Visit — & How Much You Save

Visiting in shoulder or low season can cut your costs by 20–50%. Here's the breakdown for every country in this guide.

🇹🇭
Thailand
Southeast Asia
✦ BEST: Nov–Feb Cool & dry. Peak season — book ahead.
◈ SHOULDER: Mar–May Hot but cheaper. Save 20–30% on accommodation.
✕ AVOID: Jun–Oct Monsoon season. Floods possible in south.
🇻🇳
Vietnam
Southeast Asia
✦ BEST: Feb–Apr Dry across most of the country.
◈ SHOULDER: Nov–Jan North is cooler. Save 15–25% vs peak.
✕ AVOID: Aug–Oct Typhoon season in central & north.
🇯🇵
Japan
East Asia
✦ BEST: Mar–Apr & Oct–Nov Cherry blossom & autumn foliage.
◈ SHOULDER: Jan–Feb Cold but quiet. Save 30–40% on hotels.
✕ AVOID: Golden Week (Apr 29–May 5) Prices surge 50–100%.
🇮🇳
India
South Asia
✦ BEST: Oct–Mar Cool & dry across north India.
◈ SHOULDER: Sep & Apr Transition months. Save 20–35%.
✕ AVOID: May–Aug Extreme heat (45°C+) and monsoon.
🇲🇦
Morocco
North Africa
✦ BEST: Mar–May & Sep–Nov Mild temps, ideal for desert.
◈ SHOULDER: Feb & Dec Quieter. Save 25–40% on riads.
✕ AVOID: Jul–Aug Sahara can hit 50°C. Brutal heat.
🇵🇹
Portugal
Western Europe
✦ BEST: Apr–Jun & Sep–Oct Warm, uncrowded, great prices.
◈ SHOULDER: Mar & Nov Cool but cheap. Save 30–50% vs summer.
✕ AVOID: Jul–Aug Overcrowded, 2x prices, 40°C heat.
🇨🇴
Colombia
South America
✦ BEST: Dec–Feb & Jul–Aug Dry seasons. Festivals.
◈ SHOULDER: Mar–Apr & Sep Some rain. Save 20–30%.

Travel Light, Travel Smart

The Ultimate Budget Traveller's Packing List

Everything you actually need — and what to skip. Buy locally what you can. Less luggage = no checked bag fees = more savings.

🎒
The Bag Itself
  • ✓ 40–50L backpack (carry-on friendly)
  • ✓ Small day pack (foldable)
  • ✓ Padlock (TSA-approved)
  • ✓ Dry bag / packing cubes
  • ✕ Skip: Hard suitcase — kills mobility
  • ✕ Skip: Anything over 10kg total
👕
Clothing
  • ✓ 3–4 quick-dry t-shirts
  • ✓ 1 lightweight jacket / rain layer
  • ✓ 2 pairs of pants / shorts
  • ✓ Merino wool socks (3 pairs)
  • ✓ 1 smart outfit for nicer dinners
  • ✕ Skip: Jeans — heavy & slow to dry
  • 💡 Buy thin layers locally in SE Asia — cheap & perfect for the climate
💊
Health & Safety
  • ✓ Travel insurance documents
  • ✓ Basic first aid kit
  • ✓ Diarrhoea medication (essential!)
  • ✓ Antihistamines
  • ✓ Sunscreen SPF 50+
  • ✓ DEET insect repellent
  • ✓ Prescription meds + extra supply
  • ✕ Skip: Full pharmacy — buy locally
💻
Tech & Electronics
  • ✓ Unlocked smartphone
  • ✓ Universal travel adapter
  • ✓ 20,000mAh power bank
  • ✓ Laptop (if needed) + sleeve
  • ✓ Earphones / noise-cancelling
  • ✓ USB-C charging cable (x2)
  • ✕ Skip: Travel hairdryer — hostels have them
💳
Money & Documents
  • ✓ Passport (6+ months validity)
  • ✓ Wise / Revolut card (zero FX fees)
  • ✓ Backup debit card
  • ✓ $200 USD cash emergency fund
  • ✓ Digital copies of all docs (cloud)
  • ✓ Travel insurance card
  • ✕ Skip: Traveller's cheques — useless
🌙
Comfort & Sleep
  • ✓ Silk sleeping bag liner
  • ✓ Inflatable neck pillow
  • ✓ Eye mask + ear plugs
  • ✓ Flip flops / sandals (hostel showers)
  • ✓ Quick-dry towel (microfibre)
  • ✕ Skip: Full sleeping bag unless trekking
  • 💡 A silk liner adds warmth and keeps you clean in sketchy hostel beds
💡
The Golden Rule: If in doubt, leave it out. You can buy almost anything you forget in any major city worldwide — often cheaper than at home. Toothpaste in Bangkok, a rain jacket in Hanoi, a SIM card at any airport. Overpacking costs you money in checked baggage fees and energy carrying it. The best travellers pack light and buy smart.
